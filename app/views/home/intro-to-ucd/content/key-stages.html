Key stages of the UCD process

The UCD process typically involves several key stages:

  • Defining the Context - identifying different citizen segments and conducting thorough user research such as surveys, interviews, and usability testing. This stage also ensures alignment with overall policy objectives.
  • Defining User Needs and Requirements - translating user insights into actionable requirements, prioritising features based on their impact, and setting clear success metrics for the design.
  • Co-creating and Designing Solutions - engaging citizens in design workshops and prototyping to gather feedback and refine concepts, balancing user needs with technical constraints.
  • Implementing and Refining - launching and monitoring the service, gathering feedback via analytics, and iteratively improving it based on user data.

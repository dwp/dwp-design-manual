Consider the bigger picture

First and foremost, interaction designers are problem solvers. We think about problems first and actively encourage those around us to do the same.

Interaction designers must:

  • make design decisions based on data and research
  • design out unnecessary interactions
  • design user journeys, not just screens
  • be curious and always consider different options
  • be able to take themselves out of solutions
  • design for all users regardless of ability or technology
  • positively challenge and ask why

Collaborate, share and work in the open

We facilitate discussion and help to bring understanding to teams by actively involving team members and stakeholders in the design process.

Involving others in the design process means you can get to meaningful solutions more quickly. It helps other people to feel part of the solution, builds understanding, buy in, and a sense of shared ownership. It reduces the need to explain design decisions later and makes the voice of design stronger.

Seeking feedback early and often helps identify any potential issues in a design quickly. It also helps spot opportunities for re-use.

Interaction designers must:

  • be highly collaborative, and encourage others to be
  • use common tools, processes and patterns
  • share your work constantly
  • share successes and lessons we've learnt

Actively encourage re-use

Re-using common patterns, existing design, research and data means we can bring value by saving money and getting our products and services in the hands of users more quickly.

Interaction designers must:

  • re-use existing work whenever possible
  • document your design decisions using the DWP Design Research Library
  • support the creation and improvement of patterns

Act as an ambassador for interaction design in DWP and government

We pride ourselves on being a highly supportive and inclusive community. To maintain this we all must make ourselves available to offer support to each other.

Interaction designers must:

  • be open
  • avoid jargon
  • be inclusive
  • support the community, attend community meetings, contribute to the interaction desuign slack channel
  • suggest improvements
  • build positive relationships
  • set development goals and work to develop your skills
